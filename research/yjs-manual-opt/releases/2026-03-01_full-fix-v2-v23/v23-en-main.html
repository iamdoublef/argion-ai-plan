01Safety Instructions

WARNING
  • This appliance is for household use only. Do not use it for any purpose other than its intended use.
  • Do not allow children to play with the appliance or power cord. Children, persons with reduced physical or cognitive abilities should use this appliance only under adult supervision. Keep children away during operation to prevent accidents.
  • Small parts and vacuum bags may pose a choking hazard. Keep them out of reach of children.
  • Do not use this appliance in high-humidity or high-temperature environments. Never use it in the presence of flammable or explosive gases.
  • Ensure the power supply voltage matches the rating on the appliance nameplate.
  • Do not leave the appliance unattended while in operation.
  • After sealing, the seal area is hot. Do not touch it to avoid burns.
  • Disconnect the power supply after use.
  • When unplugging, grasp the plug itself. Do not pull on the power cord.
  • Do not immerse the main unit or power cord in water or any other liquid.
  • The power cord must be plugged directly into a properly grounded electrical outlet.
  • If the plug, power cord, or main unit is damaged, stop using the appliance immediately and contact the manufacturer or an authorized service center. Do not attempt to repair it yourself.
  • Do not let the power cord hang over the edge of a table or counter, as children may pull it or someone may trip over it.
CAUTION
  • Please keep this manual for future reference.
  • This appliance does not require any lubricant. Do not use organic solvents for cleaning.
  • When not in use for an extended period, do not lock the latch, as this may cause the sealing gasket to deform under pressure.
  • Do not dispose of this product with household waste. Please recycle or dispose of it in accordance with local regulations for electronic waste.
NOTICE
  • This product is intended for household food preservation use.
  • Please read this manual completely before first use.
  • Please retain your proof of purchase for warranty service.

02Product & Vacuum Bag Tips

  • This appliance is only compatible with vacuum bags that have air-channel texture.
  • For items with sharp points or edges, wrap them with puncture-proof pads, paper towels, or other soft material to prevent puncturing the vacuum bag.
  • Vacuum bags can be used for heating or microwave reheating, but you must first cut a small opening at a corner of the bag to allow ventilation.
  • Vacuum bags used for storage can be reused, but bags used for heating or microwaving should only be used once. Bags that have stored seafood or greasy food are not recommended for reuse.
  • When placing the bag, the bag opening must lie flat across the heating element.
  • When filling the bag, always leave at least 2 inches (5 cm) of space between the food and the bag opening.
  • When packing liquids, sauces, or food containing liquid, avoid getting the bag opening wet. It is recommended to wipe the bag opening dry after filling to ensure a proper seal.
  • If you want soft, high-moisture food to maintain its shape after packaging, consider freezing it before vacuum sealing.
  • Do not place heavy objects on the top lid, as this may damage the appliance.

06Operating Instructions

6.1 How to Use the Bag Cutter
  1. 1Open the top lid.
  2. 2Open the cutter bracket.
  3. 3Pull out the desired length of bag from the roll.
  4. 4Move the bag cutter to the far left or right, then press the cutter bracket down onto the bag.
  5. 5Press down and slide the bag cutter across the bag for a clean cut.
6.2 How to Use the Seal Function

Making a bag from a roll and sealing it:

  1. 1Follow the bag cutter instructions above to cut a bag with two open ends.
  2. 2Place one open end of the bag flat into the vacuum chamber.
  3. 3Close the top lid and press the latch handle down to lock it.
  4. 4Press the Seal button and wait for the machine to finish.

Sealing a bag only:

  1. 1Place the item to be sealed into the bag, then place the bag opening into the vacuum chamber.
  2. 2Close the top lid and press the latch handle down to lock it.
  3. 3Press the Seal button and wait for the machine to finish.
6.3 How to Extend Seal Time
To extend the sealing time, press the Extend Seal Time button before starting an operation so that its indicator light turns on. The extended time setting will remain active. To restore the default sealing time, press the button again before the next operation. When the machine enters standby mode or is powered off and back on, the sealing time will reset to default.
CAUTION
When the Extend Seal Time function is enabled, the smart seal adjustment feature is temporarily disabled. During continuous operation, allow at least 20 seconds between each cycle and open the top lid to let the machine cool down.

06Operating Instructions (cont.)

6.4 How to Use the Vacuum Seal Function

Vacuum sealing solid items:

  1. 1Place the bag opening into the vacuum chamber.
  2. 2Close the top lid and press the latch handle down to lock it.
  3. 3Press the Vac Mode button to select a mode: Normal or Gentle.
  4. 4Press the Vac&Seal / Start button to begin.
  5. 5To stop the process at any time, press the Stop button.
Before using Gentle mode on valuable or delicate items, it is recommended to test with other items first to check whether the compression level is acceptable, to avoid crushing the contents.

Vacuum sealing liquids:

  1. 1Keep the bag upright. Ensure the liquid level stays below the heating element, and leave at least 7 inches (18 cm) of space between the liquid and the bag opening.
  2. 2Place the bag opening into the vacuum chamber.
  3. 3Close the top lid and press the latch handle down to lock it.
  4. 4Press the Vac Mode button to select Liquid mode.
  5. 5Press the Vac&Seal / Start button to begin.
6.5 How to Use Manual Vacuum

Method 1 (Switch to Pulse Vac during automatic vacuum):

  1. 1Press and hold the Pulse Vac button to vacuum.
  2. 2Release the button to pause vacuuming.
  3. 3Once the desired vacuum level is reached, press the Seal button to seal.

Method 2 (Use Pulse Vac directly):

  1. 1Place the bag opening into the vacuum chamber.
  2. 2Close the top lid and press the latch handle down to lock it.
  3. 3Press and hold the Pulse Vac button to vacuum.
  4. 4Release the button to pause vacuuming.
  5. 5Once the desired vacuum level is reached, press the Seal button to seal.
During manual vacuum, press and hold the button to vacuum, release to pause — the machine will hold pressure. If no further action is taken within 10 seconds, the machine will automatically release pressure and exit manual vacuum mode.

06Operating Instructions (Accessories)

6.6 Vacuum Hose Usage
  • When vacuuming mason jar adapters and valve-equipped zipper bags, use the round nozzle in orientation 1.
  • When vacuuming canisters and bottle stoppers, choose orientation 1 or 2 depending on the size of the vacuum port.
  • The flat nozzle 3 of the vacuum hose connects to the external vacuum port 3 on the top lid.
6.7 How to Vacuum a Mason Jar
  1. 1Close the top lid of the machine.
  2. 2Place the jar lid on the mason jar opening. Do not screw on the threaded ring (if applicable).
  3. 3Attach the adapter onto the mason jar.
  4. 4Insert the flat nozzle of the vacuum hose into the external vacuum port on the top lid.
  5. 5Press the latch handle down to lock the top lid.
  6. 6Press the Accessories button and select Mason Jar.
  7. 7Place the round nozzle of the vacuum hose over the adapter's vacuum port and press down gently with your hand.
  8. 8Press the Vac&Seal / Start button to begin.
  9. 9When finished, remove the vacuum hose and adapter.
6.8 How to Vacuum a Canister
  1. 1Before starting, confirm that the canister is compatible with this machine's vacuum hose.
  2. 2Ensure the canister is in the vacuum-ready (Vacuum) position.
  3. 3Insert the flat nozzle of the vacuum hose into the external vacuum port on the top lid.
  4. 4Press the latch handle down to lock the top lid.
  5. 5Press the Accessories button and select Canister.
  6. 6Place the round nozzle of the vacuum hose over the canister's vacuum valve port and press down gently.
  7. 7Press the Vac&Seal / Start button to begin.
  8. 8When finished, remove the vacuum hose.

07Troubleshooting (Liquid Ingress)

What to Do If Liquid Enters the Machine

Liquid enters the vacuum chamber:

  1. 1Immediately turn off the power and wait at least 5 minutes for the heating element to cool down.
  2. 2Remove the drip tray and clean up the liquid.
  3. 3Wipe dry any liquid in the vacuum chamber.
  4. 4Place paper towels or a tray under the machine, then reconnect the power.
  5. 5Close the top lid, press and hold the Pulse Vac button for 20 seconds, then press Stop to release air. Repeat 5 times to expel liquid from the internal tubing.
  6. 6Open the top lid, place paper towels or a tray under the machine, and leave it in a well-ventilated area to dry for 48 hours. Do not tip the machine over during this time.

Liquid enters the heating element:

  1. 1Do not tip the machine over. Turn off the power and wait 10 minutes for the heating element to cool and internal liquid to drain.
  2. 2Clean and dry the drip tray, vacuum chamber, and any liquid under the machine.
  3. 3Open the top lid, place paper towels or a tray under the machine, and leave it in a well-ventilated area to dry for 24 hours. Do not tip the machine over.
  4. 4Place paper towels or a tray under the machine, then reconnect the power.
  5. 5Close the top lid, press and hold the Pulse Vac button for 20 seconds, then press Stop to release air. Repeat 5 times to expel liquid from the internal tubing.
  6. 6Open the top lid, place paper towels or a tray under the machine, and leave it in a well-ventilated area to dry for 48 hours. Do not tip the machine over during this time.
CAUTION
If the internal tubing is not cleaned regularly, it may become clogged over time, affecting vacuum performance. In this case, please contact customer support for tubing replacement.

08Maintenance

  • Ensure the work surface is flat, clean, and dry before using the machine.
  • When the machine is not in use, do not lock the latch, as prolonged pressure may deform the sealing gasket and affect performance.
  • Clean the sealing gasket, sealing strip, heating element, drip tray, and vacuum chamber at least once a week.
  • The heating element and sealing strip remain hot after operation. Allow the machine to cool down before performing maintenance.
  • Unplug the machine before cleaning.
  • Use a soft brush to clean the sealing gasket, sealing strip, heating element, drip tray, vacuum chamber, and vacuum hose. Avoid using hard brushes, as they may damage the machine.
  • After each day of use, remove the drip tray, clean and dry it, and wipe the vacuum chamber with a damp cloth or paper towel to remove any residual debris or dust.
  • After each use, inspect the sealing gasket for significant deformation and the heat tape for damage or peeling. Replace promptly if needed.
  • The drip tray and vacuum hose are not dishwasher-safe and should not be cleaned with high-temperature methods.

09Vacuum Packaging Guide

Vacuum packaging extends the shelf life of food by removing most of the air from a sealed container and preventing air from re-entering, thereby reducing oxidation. Vacuum packaging helps preserve flavor and overall quality. It also helps inhibit the growth of aerobic microorganisms. Under certain conditions, the following may occur:

  • Mold — Mold cannot thrive in low-oxygen environments. Vacuum packaging effectively inhibits mold growth.
  • Yeast — Yeast requires water, sugar, and moderate temperatures to grow, and can survive with or without air. Refrigeration slows yeast growth; freezing effectively inhibits it.
  • Bacteria — Bacteria cause unpleasant odors, discoloration, or softening. Under certain conditions, Clostridium botulinum can grow in the absence of air and cannot be detected by smell or taste.

As with any storage method, it is important to inspect food for spoilage before consumption. At temperatures of 25 °F (-4 °C) or lower, microbial growth is significantly reduced. For long-term storage, vacuum sealing followed by freezing is recommended.

NOTICE
Vacuum packaging is not a substitute for refrigeration or freezing. Any perishable food that requires refrigeration must still be kept refrigerated or frozen after vacuum packaging.
Storage Tips
  • Vegetables should be blanched before vacuum packaging. Place vegetables in boiling water until cooked, then immerse in cold water. Pat dry with a towel before vacuum packaging.
  • Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, Brussels sprouts, cabbage, etc.) naturally release gases during storage. After cooking and vacuum sealing, store them in the refrigerator.
  • Never vacuum-store garlic, mushrooms, or other fungi. Harmful substances may develop under vacuum conditions, posing a health risk if consumed.
