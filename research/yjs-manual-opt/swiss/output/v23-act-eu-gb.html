CH.01 — SAFETY

01Safety Instructions

WARNING
  • This machine is for household use only. Do not use for any purpose other than its intended design.
  • Do not allow children to play with the machine or power cord. Children and persons with reduced physical, sensory or mental capabilities should use the machine under adult supervision. Supervise children during use to prevent accidents.
  • Small parts and vacuum bags may pose a choking hazard. Keep out of reach of children.
  • Do not use the machine in high humidity, high temperature, or other unsuitable environments. Never use in the presence of flammable or explosive gases.
  • Ensure the power supply voltage matches the rating on the machine nameplate.
  • Do not leave the machine unattended while it is operating.
  • After sealing, the seal area is very hot. Do not touch to avoid burns.
  • Disconnect the power supply after use.
  • When unplugging from the outlet, grasp the plug firmly. Do not pull on the power cord.
  • Do not immerse the main unit or power cord in water or any other liquid.
  • The power cord must be plugged directly into a properly grounded outlet that meets safety standards.
  • If the plug, power cord, or main unit is damaged, stop use immediately and contact the manufacturer or authorized service center. Do not attempt to repair yourself.
  • Do not let the power cord hang over the edge of the counter, where children could pull it or someone could trip.
CAUTION
  • Keep this manual in a safe place.
  • This machine does not require lubrication. Do not clean with organic solvents.
  • When not in use for extended periods, do not engage the latch to avoid deforming the seal gasket under pressure.
  • Do not dispose of this product with household waste. Recycle or dispose of according to your local regulations for electronic waste.
NOTICE
  • This product is intended for household food preservation.
  • Read this manual in full before first use.
  • Retain your proof of purchase for warranty service.

CH.02 — USAGE TIPS

02Product & Vacuum Bag Tips

  • This machine is only compatible with vacuum bags that have air channel grooves.
  • For items with sharp points or edges, wrap corners with puncture-resistant sheets, paper towels, or other soft materials to prevent puncturing the vacuum bag.
  • Vacuum bags may be used for heating or microwave heating, but you must first cut a small vent in a corner of the bag.
  • Vacuum bags used for storage can be reused, but bags used for heating or microwave heating should be used only once. Bags that have stored seafood or oily foods are not recommended for reuse.
  • When placing the bag, the bag opening must lie flat across the heating element.
  • When filling bags, always leave at least 2 inch (5 cm) of space between the bag opening and the food.
  • When sealing liquids, sauces, or foods with liquid, avoid wetting the bag opening. Wipe the opening dry before sealing to ensure a proper seal.
  • For soft, high-moisture foods that need to retain their shape after packaging, consider freezing before vacuum sealing.
  • Do not place heavy objects on the lid, as this may damage the machine.

CH.06 — OPERATION

06Operation Guide

6.1 How to Use the Bag Cutter
  1. 1Open the lid.
  2. 2Open the bag cutter holder.
  3. 3Pull the desired length of bag from the roll.
  4. 4Move the bag cutter to the far left or right, then press the holder down onto the bag.
  5. 5Press the bag cutter down and slide it across the bag for a clean cut.
6.2 How to Use the Seal Function

Making a bag from a roll and sealing:

  1. 1Create an open-ended bag using the bag cutter steps above.
  2. 2Place one open end of the bag flat into the vacuum chamber.
  3. 3Close the lid and press the handle down to lock.
  4. 4Press the Seal button and wait for the machine to complete.

Sealing a bag only:

  1. 1Place items in the bag and put the bag opening into the vacuum chamber.
  2. 2Close the lid and press the handle down to lock.
  3. 3Press the Seal button and wait for the machine to complete.
6.3 How to Extend Seal Time
To extend seal time, press the Extend Seal Time button before operation so its indicator lights up. The extended time setting remains in effect. To restore default seal time, press the button again before the next operation. When the machine enters sleep mode or is powered off and on again, seal time will revert to the default setting.
CAUTION
When Extend Seal Time is enabled, smart seal adjustment is temporarily disabled. Allow more than 20 seconds between consecutive operations and open the lid for cooling.

CH.06 — OPERATION (CONT.)

06Operation Guide (Cont.)

6.4 How to Use Vacuum Seal

Vacuum sealing solid items:

  1. 1Place the bag opening into the vacuum chamber.
  2. 2Close the lid and press the handle down to lock.
  3. 3Press the Vac Mode button to select Normal or Gentle mode.
  4. 4Press the Vac&Seal / Start button to start.
  5. 5To stop, press the Stop button.
Before using Gentle mode for delicate items, test with other items first to ensure the compression level meets your needs and does not crush the contents.

Vacuum sealing liquids:

  1. 1Keep the bag upright so the liquid level stays below the heating element. Leave about 7 inch (18 cm) of space between the liquid and the bag opening.
  2. 2Place the bag opening into the vacuum chamber.
  3. 3Close the lid and press the handle down to lock.
  4. 4Press the Vac Mode button to select Liquid mode.
  5. 5Press the Vac&Seal / Start button to start.
6.5 How to Use Manual Vacuum

Method 1 (Switch to Pulse Vac during auto vacuum):

  1. 1Hold the Pulse Vac button to vacuum.
  2. 2Release the button to pause.
  3. 3When the desired vacuum level is reached, press the Seal button to seal.

Method 2 (Direct Pulse Vac mode):

  1. 1Place the bag opening into the vacuum chamber.
  2. 2Close the lid and press the handle down to lock.
  3. 3Hold the Pulse Vac button to vacuum.
  4. 4Release the button to pause.
  5. 5When the desired vacuum level is reached, press the Seal button to seal.
During manual vacuum, holding the button runs the pump and releasing pauses it (pressure is maintained). If no action is taken within 10 seconds, the machine will automatically release pressure and exit manual vacuum mode.

CH.06 — ACCESSORIES

06Operation Guide (Accessories)

6.6 Vacuum Hose Usage
  • For Mason jar adapters and valve zipper bags, use the round nozzle in orientation 1.
  • For vacuum canisters and bottle stoppers, choose orientation 1 or 2 depending on the valve size.
  • The flat nozzle 3 on the vacuum hose connects to the external vacuum port 3 on the machine lid.
6.7 How to Vacuum Mason Jars
  1. 1Close the machine lid.
  2. 2Place the lid on the Mason jar mouth without screwing on the ring (if present).
  3. 3Attach the adapter to the Mason jar.
  4. 4Insert the vacuum hose flat nozzle into the external vacuum port on the machine lid.
  5. 5Press the handle down to lock the lid.
  6. 6Press the Accessories button and select Mason Jar.
  7. 7Cover the adapter vacuum port with the vacuum hose round nozzle and press gently with your hand.
  8. 8Press the Vac&Seal / Start button to start.
  9. 9When done, remove the vacuum hose and adapter.
6.8 How to Vacuum Canisters
  1. 1Before use, confirm the canister is compatible with this machine's vacuum hose.
  2. 2Confirm the canister is in Vacuum mode.
  3. 3Insert the vacuum hose flat nozzle into the external vacuum port on the machine lid.
  4. 4Press the handle down to lock the lid.
  5. 5Press the Accessories button and select Canister.
  6. 6Cover the canister valve vacuum port with the vacuum hose round nozzle and press gently.
  7. 7Press the Vac&Seal / Start button to start.
  8. 8When done, remove the vacuum hose.

CH.07 — LIQUID RECOVERY

07Troubleshooting (Cont.)

Recovery procedure if liquid enters the machine

If liquid enters the vacuum chamber:

  1. 1Turn off power immediately. Wait at least 5 minutes for the heating element to cool.
  2. 2Remove the liquid tray and wipe dry.
  3. 3Wipe the liquid from the vacuum chamber.
  4. 4Place kitchen paper or a tray under the machine and power on again.
  5. 5Close the lid, hold the Pulse Vac button for 20 seconds, then press Stop to release. Repeat 5 times to expel liquid from internal tubing.
  6. 6Open the lid, place kitchen paper or a tray under the machine, and let it air dry in a ventilated area for 48 hours. Do not tilt the machine during this time.

If liquid enters the heating element:

  1. 1Do not tilt the machine. Turn off power and wait 10 minutes for the heating element to cool and internal liquid to drain.
  2. 2Clean and wipe liquid from the drip tray, vacuum chamber, and bottom of the machine.
  3. 3Open the lid, place kitchen paper or a tray under the machine, and let it air dry in a ventilated area for 24 hours. Do not tilt the machine during this time.
  4. 4Place kitchen paper or a tray under the machine and power on again.
  5. 5Close the lid, hold the Pulse Vac button for 20 seconds, then press Stop to release. Repeat 5 times to expel liquid from internal tubing.
  6. 6Open the lid, place kitchen paper or a tray under the machine, and let it air dry in a ventilated area for 48 hours. Do not tilt the machine during this time.
CAUTION
If not cleaned regularly, internal tubing may become clogged and affect vacuum performance. Contact customer service for tubing replacement.

CH.08 — MAINTENANCE

08Maintenance

  • Ensure the work surface is flat, clean, and dry.
  • When not in use, do not engage the latch to avoid compressing the seal gasket and affecting performance.
  • Clean the seal gasket, seal bar, heating element, liquid tray, and vacuum chamber weekly.
  • The heating element and seal bar remain hot after use. Allow the machine to cool before maintenance.
  • Unplug the machine before cleaning.
  • Use a soft brush to clean the seal gasket, seal bar, heating element, liquid tray, vacuum chamber, and vacuum hose. Avoid stiff brushes to prevent damage.
  • After each day's use, remove and clean the liquid tray, wipe dry, and use a damp cloth or paper towel to remove debris and dust from the vacuum chamber.
  • After each use, check if the seal gasket is severely deformed or if the high-temperature tape is damaged or detached. Replace if needed.
  • The liquid tray and vacuum hose are not dishwasher safe.

CH.09 — VACUUM PROPERTIES

09Vacuum Packaging Properties

Vacuum packaging extends food life by removing most of the air from a sealed container and preventing air from re-entering, thereby reducing oxidation. It helps preserve flavor and overall quality. It also helps inhibit the growth of aerobic microorganisms. Under certain conditions, the following may occur:

  • Mold — Mold cannot grow in low-oxygen environments. Vacuum packaging inhibits mold growth.
  • Yeast — Yeast needs water, sugar, and moderate temperature to grow. It can survive with or without air. Refrigeration slows yeast growth; freezing effectively inhibits it.
  • Bacteria — Causes off odors, discoloration, or softening. Under the right conditions, Clostridium botulinum can grow without air and cannot be detected by smell or taste.

As with any storage method, it is important to check food for spoilage before eating. At -4°C or lower, microbial growth can be significantly reduced. For long-term storage, vacuum seal and keep frozen.

NOTICE
Vacuum packaging does not replace refrigeration or freezing. Any perishable food that requires refrigeration must remain refrigerated or frozen after vacuum packaging.
Storage Guide
  • Vegetables must be blanched before vacuum packaging. Boil in water, then immerse in cold water, and pat dry with a towel before vacuum sealing.
  • Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, sprouts, cabbage, etc.) naturally release gas during storage. After cooking and vacuum sealing, store in the refrigerator.
  • Never vacuum store garlic or mushrooms. Vacuuming can produce harmful substances that may affect health if consumed.
