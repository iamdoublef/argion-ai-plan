CH.01 — SAFETY

01Safety Instructions

For your safety, please read all instructions in this manual before using this electrical appliance and always follow these safety precautions:

WARNING
  • This product is for household use only. Do not use for any unintended purpose.
  • Do not allow children to play with this product or its power cord. Children and persons with limited mobility or cognitive ability should use it under adult supervision.
  • Small parts may pose a choking hazard. Keep away from children.
  • Do not use this product in high-humidity or high-temperature environments. Never use in the presence of flammable or explosive gases.
  • Ensure the power supply voltage matches the rating on the product nameplate.
  • Disconnect the power after use.
  • When unplugging, grip the plug itself — do not pull the power cord.
  • Do not immerse the unit or power cord in water or any other liquid.
  • The power cord must be plugged directly into a properly rated electrical outlet.
  • If the plug, power cord, or unit is damaged, stop using it immediately and contact the manufacturer or an authorized service center. Do not attempt self-repair.
  • Do not let the power cord hang over the edge of a counter — children may pull it, causing tipping or injury.
  • Never touch the product's electrical components with wet hands or bare feet.
  • The power supply connected to this product must have a residual-current device (RCD/GFCI).
  • The outlet must comply with local regulations. Safety regulations require mandatory grounding of this product.
  • Before cleaning, maintenance, or replacing parts, always disconnect the power supply.
  • Never insert screwdrivers or other objects into the ventilation openings.
  • Only authorized technicians may disassemble, inspect, or repair this product.
  • In case of fire, do not use water — use a CO₂ fire extinguisher.
  • Do not use mechanical means or any method other than those recommended by the manufacturer to accelerate defrosting.
  • Do not damage the refrigerant circuit.
  • Warning: Flammable materials — risk of fire.
  • Refrigerant waste must be disposed of at an authorized waste processing center. Keep away from open flames.
  • Do not store aerosol cans or other explosive substances containing flammable propellants inside this product.
  • Keep all ventilation openings clear. Keep the product away from heat sources or steam-generating appliances.

CH.02 — USAGE TIPS

02Product Usage Tips

Placement & First Use
  • Place the product on a sturdy, flat, dry countertop.
  • Ensure at least 120 mm (4.7") ventilation clearance on all sides.
  • Keep away from heat sources (ovens, stovetops, etc.) and direct sunlight.
  • Run the cleaning cycle once before first use (see CH.08).
  • The first 5 ice-making cycles are a cool-down phase, so smaller ice during this stage is normal.
Water Quality
  • Use drinking water for ice making. The purer the water, the clearer the ice.
  • For optimal clarity, use water with low mineral content (TDS < 200 mg/L).
  • Suitable sources: purified water, RO-filtered water, deeply filtered water, low-TDS mineral water.
  • Do NOT use colored, flavored, carbonated, or gas-saturated beverages (tea, juice, coffee, milk, soda, etc.) — these may contaminate or damage the product.
Usage Notes
  • Remove all packaging materials after unboxing, wipe the interior with a damp cloth, and dry thoroughly.
  • When the ice basket is full, the unit pauses automatically and alerts the user.
  • Only one ice size can be produced at a time, but the size can be changed during operation.
  • Occasional cloudy ice during continuous operation is normal and depends on water quality, water temperature, and ambient temperature.

06Operation Guide

Making Ice

  1. 2Place the ice basket inside the unit and close the lid.

CH.08 — MAINTENANCE

08Maintenance

Auto-Clean (Ice Mold & Tray)
  1. 1Fill the water tank to the water line.
  2. 2With the unit powered on, press the Clean button. The indicator lights white and the unit cleans automatically.
  3. 3When cleaning is complete, the unit stops automatically and the indicator turns off.
Draining
  1. 1Unplug the power cord and place the unit near a sink.
  2. 2Remove the drain plug from the bottom of the unit and let all water drain out.
  3. 3Once drained, replace the drain plug securely.
Daily Maintenance
  • Regularly rinse the interior with clean water — we recommend cleaning before each daily use.
  • Wipe the exterior with a soft, damp cloth. Do not use abrasive or corrosive cleaners.
  • Follow the auto-clean steps to clean the ice mold and tray.
  • Always disconnect power before maintenance and wait for the unit to cool down.

CH.09 — INSTALLATION / STORAGE / DISPOSAL

09Installation, Transport, Storage & Disposal

Packaging & Transport
  • Keep the unit balanced during transport to prevent tipping. The tilt angle must not exceed 45°.
  • Always keep the product upright during handling and transport to avoid compressor or refrigerant-system damage.
  • After transportation, let the unit stand upright for 4 hours before powering on.
Installation
  • Install the product on a flat, stable, and dry surface. Do not operate it while tilted.
  • Do not drag or force the product during positioning to avoid tipping or component damage.
  • Keep at least 120 mm (4.7") of clearance from walls and other objects for ventilation and service access.
  • Keep the product away from heat sources, steam, and direct sunlight.
Storage & Disposal
  • Before long-term storage, drain all water from the tank and inner cavity, then clean and dry the unit thoroughly.
  • Store the unit in a dry, ventilated environment and avoid heavy loads or moisture exposure.
  • This product contains electronic components and R600a refrigerant and must not be disposed of with household waste.
  • At end of life, contact an authorized recycling center or retailer and follow local disposal regulations.
WEEE / R600a
Do not damage the refrigerant circuit. End-of-life treatment must be handled by a qualified recycling or waste-management service to avoid environmental or safety risks.
